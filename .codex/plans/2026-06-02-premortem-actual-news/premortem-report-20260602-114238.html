Revised Plan

  1. Make one canonical civic story bundle the controlling artifact: story version, evidence, claims, edges, reviews, gate result, correction, outbox event, and export manifest.
  2. Build evidence as the trust root first: persist evidence, hash actual bytes, and record source class, collection time, license, publisher/source identity, and derivation chain.
  3. Treat claim extraction as model proposals, with human split/merge/edit/accept/reject states before verification.
  4. Turn publish-gate failures into remediation tasks tied to blocking claim IDs.
  5. Reframe the UI around record inspection: bundle integrity, policy pack version, claim/evidence/correction traversal, and export/replay controls.
  6. Pull minimum governance forward: reviewer identity, COI display, role separation, and funding/governance note in the first demo story.
  7. Collapse service ambition until the full loop works; preserve contracts but optimize around the first complete artifact.
  8. Name and demonstrate the category as a conformance-backed civic record node, not AI fact checking.

Pre-Launch Checklist

  • A single end-to-end test executes ingest -> extract -> review -> publish -> correct -> export -> replay from a clean database.
  • Evidence hashes are computed from actual content bytes and provenance includes source class, collection timestamp, source/publisher identity, license, and derivation chain.
  • Gate failure responses identify blocking claim IDs and actionable remediation steps.
  • The public story page exposes claim/evidence/correction traversal, policy pack version, and export/replay affordances without hiding them behind an optional panel.
  • The demo story includes at least one reviewer, one COI/governance disclosure, one correction event, and one independently replayable bundle manifest.

Failure Investigators

1. No Complete Civic Story Bundle

The project kept improving protocol language, contracts, and roadmap phrasing, but no one forced one city-council-style story through the whole system. By October, every planning conversation returned to the missing loop.

Assumption: A strong protocol scaffold would naturally converge into a real story bundle.

Watch: No fixture contains story, evidence, claims, reviews, corrections, publish metrics, and export/replay.

Likelihood
High

2. Shallow Evidence Provenance

The evidence service shipped quickly but hashed URIs or metadata instead of actual content. The gate counted evidence edges, but the evidence identity was too weak for public scrutiny.

Assumption: Content-addressed evidence is a simple storage feature.

Watch: Hashes come from URI strings; provenance omits source class, collection time, license, and derivation chain.

Damage
Severe

3. AI Claim Extraction Theater

Demos looked strong, but real civic material produced bad claim splits, missing time bounds, weak entity tags, and interpretation/factual confusion. Reviewers spent more time repairing than verifying.

Assumption: Claim extraction is mostly a model capability problem.

Watch: Reviewers edit or discard more than 30 percent of extracted claims before verification.

Likelihood
High

4. Brittle Publish Gate

The SQL gate stayed elegant but operationally awkward. Users got metrics without knowing what to fix, and public-interest urgency had no object type short of publish or block.

Assumption: Deterministic publication logic is enough if the algorithm is correct.

Watch: Gate failures do not name blocking claims or remediation steps.

Likelihood
Medium-high

5. Feed-Shaped Product Drift

The UI led with a feed and article pages. Users treated the platform like another principled news site; the record substrate became an optional expansion panel.

Assumption: Users will understand the record model if the verification spine is available somewhere.

Watch: Feedback talks about articles, balance, and audience growth instead of inspectable records.

Likelihood
Medium-high

6. Institutional Model Stays Aspirational

The product said public service, but verifier roles, COI display, funding discipline, and governance decisions were not live primitives. Civic partners saw technical promise but institutional uncertainty.

Assumption: Public-service legitimacy can be added after the substrate works.

Watch: COI, role separation, reviewer identity, and funding remain Phase 3 concerns.

Damage
High

7. Architecture Over Workflow

The service map looked professional, but the core workflow was not stable. Local service wins accumulated while the end-to-end civic loop remained broken.

Assumption: Service decomposition reduces complexity before the core workflow is proven.

Watch: Per-service tests pass, but no single test covers the whole civic record lifecycle.

Likelihood
High

8. Category Capture By Adjacent Products

Claim tools, credibility labels, and provenance products improved their slices. Without a conformance-backed local node, outsiders framed The Actual News as another AI fact checker.

Assumption: The market will understand the category distinction without a working artifact.

Watch: Partner conversations center on AI fact checking, trust scores, or balanced news.

Likelihood
Medium
