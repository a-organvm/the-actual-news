## Cross-Lens Motifs

- Record over feed: Scope, Logic, Mythos, Bridge, and Meta all converge on durable public record as the center of gravity.
- Gate over ranking: Logic, Mythos, and Bridge all treat publication as a threshold event governed by evidence, not a distribution event governed by engagement.
- Ledger over label: Scope, Logic, and Bridge all distinguish the repo from site ratings and transparency labels. Labels describe trust; ledgers produce inspectable trust objects.

## Three Meta-Patterns

1. Trust becomes mechanical before it becomes social.

   Scope names the verification spine; Logic decomposes gate conditions; Bridge maps this to maintenance logs and bookkeeping; Meta calls for replayable story bundles. The pattern is that social trust is downstream of mechanical inspectability.

2. The ideal object is a bundle, not an article.

   Logic defines narrative plus claim/evidence/correction objects; Mythos sees the docket; Bridge sees balanced books; Meta asks for a single civic story bundle. The article is only the visible face of a multi-object record.

3. The missing market category is public-interest verification infrastructure.

   Scope distinguishes standards, ratings, fact-checking, and provenance; Logic notes the repo tries to make them native; Bridge points to interconnection; Meta demands conformance. The gap is not another consumer trust score. It is infrastructure for producing, exchanging, and auditing verified civic information.

## Topological Reading

The topic's shape is a three-layer topology:

- Inner layer: technical invariants - immutable versions, content-addressed evidence, deterministic gates, append-only corrections.
- Middle layer: newsroom workflow - evidence-first drafting, claim extraction, human review, contradiction handling, public APIs.
- Outer layer: institution - funding without clicks, governance separation, federation, procurement, public archives.

The repo currently has a strong inner-layer skeleton, a partial middle-layer skeleton, and mostly aspirational outer-layer design. The next useful move is to bind all three layers in one narrow civic loop.
