## Five Lines

1. Product ontology: what is the thing being built?
2. Verification architecture: what makes truth claims inspectable?
3. Implementation gap: what exists in the repo versus what is asserted?
4. Market/space fit: what does the current space already cover?
5. Institutional design: what should exist for this to matter beyond a demo?

## Recursive Tree

- Product ontology
  - Why is this not a normal news site?
    - Because the repo defines a story as a bundle: narrative, claims, evidence edges, and corrections.
      - Therefore the product is not "content"; it is a public record artifact with a readable front end.
  - How does that change the reader relationship?
    - The reader does not merely trust a masthead; the reader inspects objects.
      - Therefore bias disputes become claim/evidence disputes rather than brand-level tribal disputes.
  - What if the platform is only a protocol?
    - Then the public-web app is a reference reader, not the center of gravity.
      - Therefore conformance, export, and federation become more important than editorial polish.

- Verification architecture
  - Why claim ledgers?
    - A story's factual surface must be decomposed into independently reviewable units.
      - Therefore extraction quality, claim granularity, and version binding become critical governance problems.
  - How should evidence work?
    - Evidence must be content-addressed, provenance-rich, and typed by source class.
      - Therefore URLs alone are insufficient; primary records, media provenance, archived blobs, and hashes matter.
  - What if claims conflict?
    - Contradiction should be a first-class object that creates review tasks and can block publication.
      - Therefore the system should preserve tension rather than hide uncertainty.

- Implementation gap
  - Why is the repo not yet the ideal?
    - The docs and protocol are ahead of runtime services.
      - Phase 0 exists, but Phase 1 end-to-end civic reporting is not implemented.
  - How does current code support the thesis?
    - Gateway has feed/story/publish endpoints and deterministic gate logic; schema and conformance harness encode invariants.
      - This proves the core ledger/gate model can be tested.
  - What remains stubbed?
    - Claim extraction, evidence registration, review persistence, auth, outbox emission, contradiction detection, and evidence graph UX are not yet production-grade.
      - Therefore the next implementation milestone must be one complete local civic story through the full loop.

- Market/space fit
  - Why is The Actual News differentiated?
    - Trust Project-style standards expose transparency practices; NewsGuard-style systems rate publishers; LaPruv/Factward-style tools verify claims; C2PA/Project Origin handles media provenance.
      - The Actual News attempts to make all of these native to publication.
  - How is the space converging?
    - It is moving toward "show your work": transparent sources, claim scoring, audit trails, content credentials, and correction visibility.
      - The repo's premise is directionally aligned with 2026 trust infrastructure.
  - What if competitors add publish gates?
    - Then the moat cannot be "AI fact checking"; it must be protocol compliance, civic workflow depth, public-interest governance, and interoperable story bundles.

- Institutional design
  - Why does economics matter?
    - If revenue rewards clicks, verification becomes a cost center.
      - Therefore funding must reward verified-information-throughput, not traffic.
  - How should governance be separated?
    - Editorial selection, verification, distribution, and funding should have separate permissions and audit trails.
      - Therefore role separation and conflict disclosures are core product primitives, not compliance afterthoughts.
  - What if a single operator controls the record?
    - The system recreates media trust dependency under a technical veneer.
      - Therefore federation, exportability, and independent conformance are required for the ideal form.

## Strongest Branch

The strongest branch is product ontology: The Actual News should be understood as a public-record compiler with a news interface. That framing disciplines everything else. It explains why the repo needs protocols, conformance tests, immutable versions, evidence hashes, correction events, and non-click economics. It also exposes the current gap: the repo has a promising record substrate, but not yet the complete civic reporting loop that would make the substrate socially legible.
