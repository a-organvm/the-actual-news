## Five Framings

1. The civic scriptorium.
2. The court record of the public square.
3. The bridgekeeper at the publish gate.
4. The repaired newspaper whose ink remembers.
5. The public utility meter for truth-work.

## Stories

- The civic scriptorium: Scribes no longer copy royal proclamations or merchant notices. They bind every sentence to the document, witness, dataset, or recording from which it came. The monastery is not sacred because its writers are pure; it is trusted because every marginal note remains visible.

- The court record of the public square: The story becomes testimony, the claim becomes an exhibit, the evidence edge becomes admissibility, and the correction becomes an amended filing. The reader is not asked to clap for a verdict; the reader can inspect the docket.

- The bridgekeeper at the publish gate: A story approaches the bridge to public visibility. The bridgekeeper does not ask whether the story is popular or dramatic. It asks whether contradicted claims remain, whether primary evidence exists, and whether dangerous claims have independent support.

- The repaired newspaper whose ink remembers: Ordinary media acts as if yesterday's edition can be silently overwritten. This system makes the ink remember the scrape, the repair, and the reason. A correction is not shame hidden in the archive; it is part of the object's visible life.

- The public utility meter for truth-work: The city does not buy water because a pipe is exciting. It buys reliable flow. The Actual News imagines verified information as civic infrastructure: measured, maintained, audited, and paid for because public life needs it.

## Archetypal Reading

- Scribe: disciplined recording over charisma.
- Judge: rules of admission over narrative force.
- Threshold guardian: deterministic gate before public consequence.
- Healer/restorer: corrections as repair rather than concealment.
- Steward: public utility governance over private attention extraction.

The central myth is not "the heroic journalist reveals truth." It is "the city learns to keep a record that can withstand its own disagreements."
