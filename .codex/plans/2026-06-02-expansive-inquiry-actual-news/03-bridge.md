## Five Bridges

- Aviation maintenance logs: Airworthiness is not a vibe; it is a documented chain of inspections, parts, failures, and sign-offs. Newsworthiness in this repo should become "public-record-worthiness": a story is publishable only when its claim/evidence/correction maintenance log meets policy.

- Food safety HACCP: Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points does not inspect quality only at the end. It identifies failure points throughout production. The Actual News should treat unsupported claims, weak provenance, conflicted reviewers, and untracked corrections as critical control points.

- Double-entry bookkeeping: Trust emerges because every debit has a credit and the books can be audited. Every narrative assertion should have a corresponding ledger object: claim, evidence edge, uncertainty, or correction. Unbalanced narrative is technical debt.

- Clinical trials registry: A result is more trustworthy when protocol, enrollment, endpoints, amendments, and adverse events are public. Investigations should have preregistered scope, evidence collection logs, review thresholds, and visible amendments.

- Electrical grid interconnection: A power grid needs shared standards so many producers can interoperate without trusting one operator. News bundles should be exportable, signed, replayable, and federated so local nodes can exchange verified records.

## Hybrid Questions

- Aviation: What would a "story airworthiness certificate" contain before public release?
- Food safety: Which claim-production hazards should block publication automatically?
- Bookkeeping: What is the minimal balanced transaction for every sentence in a narrative?
- Clinical trials: When should an investigation declare its protocol before reporting begins?
- Electrical grid: What conformance tests let independent news nodes exchange story bundles safely?

## Most Productive Bridge

Double-entry bookkeeping is the strongest bridge. It makes the repo's ideal painfully concrete: narrative is one side of the books; claims, evidence, review, and corrections are the other. A story with unsupported factual assertions is not merely "low quality"; it is out of balance.
