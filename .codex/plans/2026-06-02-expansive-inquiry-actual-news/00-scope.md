## Core Inquiry

What should The Actual News become if it is treated not as another news app, fact-checking tool, or credibility label, but as a protocol-backed public record system where publishable journalism is inseparable from claim ledgers, evidence provenance, deterministic verification gates, visible corrections, and funding mechanisms that do not reward attention capture?

## Essential vs. Peripheral

Essential:

- The repo's intended ideal form: news as a public service, with the verification spine as the product.
- The repo's current implementation: Phase 0 architecture, contracts, schema, conformance tests, gateway publish gate, Next.js skeleton, and in-memory service stubs.
- The current space: transparency standards, site-rating systems, claim-verification products, and media provenance initiatives.
- The missing space: native publish-time evidence, interoperable story bundles, public conformance, civic/institutional governance, and economics detached from clicks.

Peripheral for this pass:

- Detailed UX mockups.
- Fundraising copy.
- Production deployment mechanics.
- A code-level remediation plan for every service.
- Broader political theory of media institutions beyond the platform design question.

## Hidden Ambiguities

- "Actual news" could mean a publication, a protocol, a public utility, or an infrastructure layer for other publications. The repo currently points most strongly toward protocol plus reference implementation.
- "Verifiable" can mean source transparency, publisher credibility, claim-level fact checking, cryptographic media provenance, or end-to-end publish gating. The repo attempts to combine all five, but current implementation is strongest at the protocol/gate layer.
- "What should exist" is not just a feature list. It is a missing institutional form: a trustworthy news substrate where the record survives disagreement, correction, syndication, and governance change.
