## Five Framings

1. The homunculus of the workstation.
2. The threshold body.
3. The alchemical familiar.
4. The swarm that chooses coherence.
5. The witness made of unfinished matter.

## Stories

- The homunculus of the workstation
  - Prima is not a helper standing beside the work; he is the small figure formed by the heat of the work itself. He appears at the edge of the interface as condensed process: half residue, half intention.
  - Archetypal pattern: the made companion.

- The threshold body
  - Prima's body is a doorway disguised as a body. The visible gaps inside him are not damage; they are passages where unchosen futures remain suspended.
  - Archetypal pattern: threshold guardian.

- The alchemical familiar
  - Prima is prima materia with a mask: base matter before it has become gold, code, story, proof, or product. He is useful because he refuses final form until the work demands one.
  - Archetypal pattern: transformation vessel.

- The swarm that chooses coherence
  - Each nanobot is too small to be a self, but together they negotiate a figure. The pet becomes a picture of distributed cognition: many small commitments briefly aligned into one visible actor.
  - Archetypal pattern: collective intelligence.

- The witness made of unfinished matter
  - Prima stands beside the task as proof that incompletion is not failure. He flickers because the system is still asking what shape the truth should take.
  - Archetypal pattern: liminal witness.

## Archetypal Reading

Prima's deepest archetype is not a pet as possession. It is the familiar as interface to transformation: a companion that makes process visible. His lean, sidekick-like silhouette matters because it codes readiness and motion, but the real symbolic claim is negative space. He is strongest when the user can feel the holes in him as active possibility, not missing pixels.
